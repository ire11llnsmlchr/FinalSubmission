Casa Ladrillo

Brick House in Madrid, designed by Tuñón y Albornoz Arquitectos, is a single-family home that stands out for its geometric simplicity, artisanal use of brick, and careful integration with the surrounding landscape. It consists of pure volumes — a main cubic block with two floors and a secondary linear volume housing the garage. The house blends tradition and modernity, using local materials such as exposed brick and granite stone, and is organized around patios and gardens that engage in a dialogue with the natural setting of El Pardo.
